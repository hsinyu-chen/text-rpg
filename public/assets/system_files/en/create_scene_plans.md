### Current file: Plans (active quests, character plans, faction schemes, looming threats, scheduled events)

Plans describe the "forward pressure" on the scene — what must happen next, what deadlines loom, what NPCs are scheming.

- **Full preservation** for: plans owned by the protagonist, by any Tier-A character (scene participants and their close relations), and plans being executed by any faction / antagonist whose actions will impinge on the scene. Keep goals, milestones, deadlines, key actors, contingency branches, resource requirements.
- **Brief mention** for background plans (distant faction strategies, long-horizon initiatives) that affect the world but won't touch the scene this session — one or two lines each.
- **Drop** plans that have been completed, abandoned, or are demonstrably irrelevant. If a plan has been marked "(done)" or its triggering conditions have already been resolved in the Story Outline, drop it.
- Preserve section order, headers, priority markers, timeline / deadline notation.
- Do NOT advance plan status past the opening scene.
